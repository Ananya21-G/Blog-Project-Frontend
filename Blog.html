In the modern world of software development, speed is everything. We deploy continuously, iterate rapidly, and measure success in sprints.

This revelation didn't come during a hackathon or a product launch. It came on a quiet Tuesday evening, as I sat refactoring a piece of code I'd written weeks earlier.

The Tyranny of Speed

We live in an age where productivity is measured in velocity points and success is quantified by lines of code per day.

The faster we move, the less we see. The more we build, the less we understand what we've built.

I've watched technical debt accumulate like sediment in a river...
